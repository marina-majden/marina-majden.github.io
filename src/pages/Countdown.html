The 1% Theory

The Great Synchronization is a global experiment in collective attention. The underlying idea refers to the "1% theory" (often linked sociologically to the Maharishi Effect), which posits that if just one percent of a population concentrates on a unified positive thought, it can profoundly shift the collective state of mind.

Scientifically speaking, while instantaneous quantum telepathy remains speculative, the sociological impact is very real: synchronized global movements create powerful cultural shifts, fostering compassion and unity across borders. When millions focus on the same positive outcome, it alters global communication, digital footprint, and shared cultural behavior.

We aim to reach roughly 80 million people (approx. 1% of the global population) to stop and focus on Peace, Love, Unity, and Respect for just five minutes.
